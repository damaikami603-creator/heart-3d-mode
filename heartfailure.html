Heart Failure

With heart failure, your heart doesn't pump blood as well as it should to meet your body's needs. Symptoms include shortness of breath, fatigue, and swelling.

Healthy Heart vs. Heart Failure

A healthy heart efficiently pumps oxygen-rich blood to the body and returns oxygen-poor blood to the lungs. In contrast, an unhealthy heart — particularly in heart failure (also called congestive heart failure) — cannot pump enough blood to meet the body's needs. Heart failure often develops from conditions like coronary artery disease, high blood pressure, previous heart attacks, or cardiomyopathies (especially dilated cardiomyopathy).
