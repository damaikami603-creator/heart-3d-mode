Arrhythmia

Arrhythmia is a type of heart condition where the heart rhythm is not steady. It causes disruptions in the heartâ€™s normal rhythm. The heart may beat too slowly, too quickly, or irregularly.Arrhythmia, also known as cardiac arrhythmia or irregular heartbeat, refers to any abnormality in the rate or rhythm of the heart's beating. Normally, the heart maintains a steady rhythm controlled by electrical signals starting from the sinoatrial (SA) node in the right atrium, which acts as the natural pacemaker, spreading through the atria to the atrioventricular (AV) node and then to the ventricles via specialized conduction pathways to produce coordinated contractions. In arrhythmia, these electrical impulses become disrupted, causing the heart to beat too fast (tachycardia, over 100 beats per minute), too slow (bradycardia, under 60 beats per minute), or irregularly, which can affect efficient blood pumping throughout the body.
