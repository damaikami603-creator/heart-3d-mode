Welcome to Heart Health

The human heart is an essential, fist-sized, muscular organ located in the chest slightly to the left of the breastbone, serving as the core of the cardiovascular system. Working continuously 24/7, it acts as a double pump, responsible for circulating blood, oxygen, and nutrients to tissues while removing carbon dioxide and waste products. The heart beats approximately 100,000 times a day, pumping roughly 5 to 6 quarts of blood every minute, which equals about 2,000 gallons per day. Structurally, it is a hollow organ divided into four chambers—the upper right/left atria (receiving chambers) and lower right/left ventricles (pumping chambers)—separated by a muscular wall called the septum. Four one-way valves (tricuspid, pulmonary, mitral, and aortic) control the direction of blood flow to ensure it moves forward and not backward.
